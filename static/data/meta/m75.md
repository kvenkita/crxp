### Why is this important?
Nighttime-light radiance captures light pollution, which affects ecosystems, human health, and energy use, and also tracks the intensity of human development. A higher value generally signals greater need.

### About the Data
Area-weighted mean nighttime-light radiance (nW/cm²/sr) per tract from the Earth Observation Group's VIIRS annual composites (2014–2024). Higher values indicate more artificial light at night (light pollution). A satellite measurement, not a survey, so it carries no sampling margin of error.

_**Source**: Earth Observation Group, VIIRS Nighttime Lights (VNL V2)._
