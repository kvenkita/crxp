## Residential stability
Residents who lived in the same home one year ago

### Why is this important?
The share of residents who lived in the same home a year ago is a widely used proxy for residential stability and rootedness — conditions associated with stronger social ties, local knowledge, and civic participation. It is an indirect measure: it reflects who stays put, not voting, volunteering, or membership, which the ACS does not collect. High mobility is not inherently negative (it can signal opportunity or housing churn), so read it in context. Higher and lower values are not inherently better or worse.

### About the Data
Calculated from the U.S. Census Bureau's American Community Survey 5-Year Estimates (2014–2024), at the Census-tract level, with margins of error. ACS 5-year vintages overlap year to year, so the series is a rolling estimate; compare only non-overlapping periods.

_**Source**: U.S. Census Bureau, American Community Survey 5-Year Estimates._
