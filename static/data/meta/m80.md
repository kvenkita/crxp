## Creative occupations
Employed residents working in arts, design, entertainment, sports, and media occupations

### Why is this important?
The share of workers in arts, design, entertainment, sports, and media occupations is the standard Census proxy for the local "creative economy" — a workforce that drives cultural vitality, placemaking, and the experiences that make neighborhoods distinctive. It measures where creative workers live (not where arts venues or audiences are), so read it alongside cultural-amenity measures. Higher and lower values are not inherently better or worse.

### About the Data
Calculated from the U.S. Census Bureau's American Community Survey 5-Year Estimates (2024–2024), at the Census-tract level, with margins of error. ACS 5-year vintages overlap year to year, so the series is a rolling estimate; compare only non-overlapping periods.

_**Source**: U.S. Census Bureau, American Community Survey 5-Year Estimates._
