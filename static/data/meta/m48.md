## No leisure-time physical activity (adults)
Share of adults reporting no leisure-time physical activity

### Why is this important?
Physical inactivity drives obesity and chronic disease and reflects access to parks, safe streets, and time for recreation. A higher value generally signals greater need.

### About the Data
Model-based small-area estimates from the CDC PLACES project (2023–2023), which uses BRFSS survey data and multilevel regression & poststratification to estimate adult prevalence for every Census tract. Each estimate has a confidence interval; values with high uncertainty are flagged.

_**Source**: CDC PLACES (model-based small-area estimates)._
